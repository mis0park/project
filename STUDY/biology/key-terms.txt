## Topic 2.2

[surface-area-to-volume ratio]
* affects the ability of a biological system to
   - obtain necessary nutrients
   - eliminate waste products
   - acquire or disspate thermal energy
   - exchange chemicals and energy with its environment

> EQ for volume/SA of sphere, cube, rectangular solid, cylinder
> using radius, length, height, width, s = sidelength of cube
$$
\text{V_{sphere}: } V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \\
\text{V_{cube}: } V=s^3 \\
\text{V_{rectangular solid}: } V=lwh \\
\text{V_{cylinder}: } V=\pi r^2 h \\
\text{SA_{sphere}: } SA=4\pi r^2 \\
\text{SA_{cube}: } V=6s^2 \\
\text{SA_{rectangular solid}: } SA=2lh + 2lw + 2wh \\
\text{SA_{cylinder}: } SA = 2\pi rh + 2\pi r^2 \\
$$

$$
r = \text{radius} \\
l = \text{length} \\
h = \text{height} \\
w = \text{width} \\
s = \text{length of one side of a cube} \\
$$

<<<SA of the plasma membrane must be large enough to adequately exchange materials>>>
[SA-to-V ratio]
   * can restrict cell size & shape
   * smaller cells = higher SA-to-V ratio = efficient exchange of materials with environment
   * increase in volume of cells = decrease in SA-to-V ratio
      = demand for resources increases
         ===> <<<complex cellular structures (e.g., folds) are necessary to adaquately exchange materials with the environment>>>
      = affects properties like rate of heat exchange with the environment
         * smaller amounts of mass = exchange proportionally more heat with environment
         * increase in mass = SA-to-V ratio and rate of heat exchange decrease
- metabolic rate per unit body mass & size of multicelluar organisms
   - smaller size = higher metabolic rate per unit body mass

### [plasma membrane]

   - [selective permeability] = characteristic of the plasma membrane resulting from the fact that the plasma membrane contains a hydrophobic interior

| type of molecule | examples | permeability |
|------------------|-----------|-------------|
| small nonpolar molecules | $\text{N}_2$, $\text{O}_2$, $\text{CO}_2$ | freely pass across the membrane |
| hydrophilic molecules | large polar molecules, ions | move through embedded channels and transport proteins |
| ions, polar molecules |  | movement prevented by the nonpolar hydrocarbon tails of phospholipids |
| small polar, uncharged molecules | $\text{H}_2\text{O}$, $\text{NH}_3$ (ammonia) | pass through the membrane in small amounts |

## Topic 2.4
### [selective permeability]
   * function = allows for the formation of cocentration gradients of solutes across the membrane
   - [concentration gradients]

> maintain water & solute balance
[passive transport] = net movement of molecules from regions of high concentration to regions of low concentration without the direct input of metabolic energy
[active transport] = sometimes utilized to move molecules from regions of low concentration to reigions of high concentration, requires the direct input of energy to move molecules

> transport large molecules across the plasma membrane
[endocytosis] & [exocytosis] = 
   * require energy
   * move large substances or large amounts of substances
   * into and out of cells

   - [endocytosis] = the cell **takes in** large molecules and particulate matter ^by^ folding the plasma membrane on itself and forming new (small) vesicles that engulf material from the external environment
   - [exocytosis] = internal vesicles **release material** from cells ^by^ fusing with the plasma membrane and secreting large molecules from the cell

## Topic 2.6

### [facilitated diffusion] = requires transport proteins or channel proteins to enable the movement of charged ions across the membrane
   * enables the movement of large polar molecules through membranes with no energy input
   * substances move down the concentration gradient

- movement of ions across the membrane may result in polarization of membrane
- charged ions like $\text{Na}^{+}$(sodium ion) and $\text{K}^{+}$(potassium ion) require channel proteins
- [aquaporins] = channel protein used to transport large quantities of water across membranes
   * used in [facilitated diffusion]/[passive transport]

## Membrane Transport
Membrane Transport
├─ Passive transport
│  ├─ Simple diffusion
│  │   └─ small, nonpolar molecules (O₂, CO₂)
│  ├─ Osmosis
│  │   └─ water (often via aquaporins)
│  └─ Facilitated diffusion
│      ├─ channel proteins
│      │   └─ ions (Na⁺, K⁺, Cl⁻), water
│      └─ carrier proteins
│          └─ large polar molecules (e.g., glucose)
│
├─ Active transport
│  ├─ Primary active transport
│  │   └─ ATP-driven pumps (Na⁺/K⁺ pump, ATPase)
│  └─ Secondary active transport
│      └─ cotransport (uses ion gradients)
│
└─ Bulk transport (vesicular; requires energy)
   ├─ Endocytosis
   │   ├─ Phagocytosis (solids)
   │   ├─ Pinocytosis (fluids)
   │   └─ Receptor-mediated endocytosis
   └─ Exocytosis

## Prokaryote vs Eukaryote
| prokaryotes | eukaryotes |
|-------------|------------|
| x have nucleus | has a nucleus |
| circular DNA, in the nucleoid region | linear DNA, in the nucleus |
| lack internal membrane-bound organelles | membrane-bound organelles exist |
| (but) have internal regions with specialized function and structure | maintain internal membranes partition the cell into specialized regions | 
| ribosomes exist, (smaller in size) | ribosomes exist, (larger in size) |
| smaller in size | bigger in size |

eukaryotes = maintain internal membranes that partition the cell into specialized regions
